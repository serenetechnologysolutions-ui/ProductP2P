## 1. Product Overview

ProcureTrack is a Procure-to-Pay (P2P) platform that manages the full purchasing lifecycle end-to-end — from raising and approving internal requisitions, through sourcing and vendor onboarding, to issuing purchase orders, processing shipments, running compliance audits, and benchmarking prices — while layering decision intelligence (insights, alerts, exception detection, traceability) on top of the transactional flow rather than leaving procurement teams to work it out from raw records.

### Modes

- **Basic Mode** — Vendor onboarding, purchase requisitions, sourcing (RFQ), item master, vendor contracts, purchase order management, shipment tracking (ASN), document intelligence, and master data.
- **Advanced Mode** — Everything in Basic, plus supplier audits, ticketing, risk scoring, ESG tracking, and price benchmarking.

A cross-cutting **Governance layer** (configurable approval workflows, a unified document repository, and admin-configurable data-entry rules) and a **Decision Intelligence layer** (price benchmarking, vendor scoring, exception detection, document traceability) are available regardless of mode.

### User Roles

| Role | Who They Are |
|------|-------------|
| System Admin | Manages the platform itself — users, settings, and feature activation |
| MDM Admin | Manages vendor lifecycle, reviews onboarding submissions, oversees advanced modules and governance |
| Procurement Admin | Runs sourcing events, validates shipments, runs audits, manages tickets, tracks risk and pricing |
| Vendor | Self-onboards, responds to RFQs, creates shipment notices, participates in tickets |

`mdm_admin` acts as a top-level override on every approval/workflow step in the platform (it can act on a step regardless of which role the step is assigned to) — this is enforced server-side, not just hidden in the UI.

## 2. End-to-End Process Flows

This section documents how documents actually move through the system — the sequence of steps, the decision points, and which service computes each decision. Where a step exists in the backend but has no frontend surface yet, it's marked **(API only)** — see also [§6](#6-backend-capabilities-without-a-frontend-yet) for the consolidated list.

### 2.1 Master Flow — Procure to Pay

```
Purchase Requisition (PR)
   │  sourcing_strategy decided by ProcurementInsightsService-adjacent
   │  computeSourcingRecommendation() in pr.helpers.js
   ▼
   ├─ RFQ_REQUIRED ──────► RFQ (one or more rounds) ──► Award ──► Purchase Order(s)
   ├─ DIRECT_PO_ALLOWED ─────────────────────────────────────────► Purchase Order
   ├─ AUTO_PO (preferred vendor on file) ─────────────────────────► Purchase Order (auto-created)
   └─ CONTRACT_BASED (active contract) ───────────────────────────► Purchase Order (vendor/price from contract)
                                                                          │
                                                                          ▼
                                                              Advance Shipment Notice (ASN)
                                                                          │
                                                          ┌───────────────┴───────────────┐
                                                          ▼                               ▼
                                              Goods Receipt Note (GRN)              Invoice (formalized
                                              — tolerance-checked against            from the ASN's own
                                              what the ASN said was shipped          invoice fields, 3-way
                                                                                      matched vs PO + GRN)
                                                          │                               │
                                                          └───────────────┬───────────────┘
                                                                          ▼
                                                          ASN posts to ERP (mock) — gated on a
                                                          completed GRN + a matched Invoice
```

Every document in this chain carries a `transaction_chain_id`, propagated from whichever document is the root of the chain (a PR if the chain started there, an RFQ/PO if created standalone) down through every downstream document — this is what `GET /api/traceability/:documentId` walks to answer "show me everything related to this document," rendered on the **Document Traceability** page (`/traceability`, see §5 #17) as a clickable Document Flow diagram plus a chronological timeline. The chain now extends through GRN and Invoice too, not just PR/RFQ/PO/ASN — either one's own id can be used as the lookup, since both carry their own `transaction_chain_id` copied from the parent ASN at creation.

### 2.2 Purchase Requisition → Approval Flow

1. **Create** (`POST /pr`) — department, cost center, justification, line items (optionally from Item Master).
2. **Submit** (`POST /pr/:id/submit`):
   - Computes `total_value` from line items.
   - If `total_value` exceeds the `pr_rfq_threshold_value` setting and `sourcing_strategy` isn't already `RFQ_REQUIRED` → **hard-blocked** (must select RFQ sourcing).
   - **Budget check** (`computeBudgetStatus`) against the cost center's `budget_allocations` row for the current fiscal year — `within_budget` / `exceeds_budget` / `not_configured`. A breach either hard-stops the submit (`pr_budget_enforcement = hard`) or goes through as a tracked **Exception** (`budget_breach`, soft mode).
   - **Line-Level Approval flagging** — every line is freshly evaluated against `pr_line_approval_value_threshold` (default ₹2,00,000) and `pr_line_approval_categories`; lines that match are flagged `requires_line_approval = true` and reset to `pending`.
   - **Workflow selection** — `resolveApprovalRule()` picks the most specific active `pr_approval_rules` row (by document_type/department/value range) to get a `workflow_id`.
   - **Workflow instance creation** — `createWorkflowInstance()` opens the first *applicable* wave of that workflow's steps, evaluating each step's `condition_rule` (if any) against `{ total_value, category: document_type, vendor_risk_level }`. If literally every step is conditional and none apply, the instance resolves itself immediately with no human step — see step 4 below for what happens next in that case.
3. **Approve** (`POST /pr/:id/approve`), one wave at a time:
   - Only the step's `approver_role` (or `mdm_admin`) may act.
   - If the wave is **parallel** (`is_parallel`), every approver in that wave must approve before the instance advances — any single rejection fails the whole instance immediately.
   - Before the *last* wave can finalize, a check runs: **if this approval would complete the workflow and any line still needs individual review** (`requires_line_approval = true` and unresolved), the approval is rejected with a clear error — the approver must use Line-Level Approval first.
   - Each line is approved/rejected individually via `PUT /pr/:id/lines/:lineId/approve|reject` (visible as its own "Line Approval" column on the PR's Line Items tab). A rejected line is permanently excluded from sourcing.
4. **Finalize** (`finalizePrApproval`, triggered from the last approval wave, from a workflow that resolved with zero steps, or from resolving the last blocking line on such a zero-step PR):
   - Any line still `pending` (never required individual review) is bulk-approved as a bookkeeping step.
   - If **every** line ended up approved → PR status = `approved`, budget committed for the full value.
   - If **some but not all** lines were rejected → PR status = `partially_approved`, budget committed only for the approved lines' value.
   - If **every** line was rejected → PR status = `rejected`.
   - **AUTO_PO** sourcing strategy with a preferred vendor on file auto-creates the PO right here, for a fully (not partially) approved PR.
5. **Reject** (`POST /pr/:id/reject`) — available at any step, ends the requisition immediately with a mandatory reason.
6. **Edit** (`PUT /pr/:id`) — **draft/rejected only** *(tightened this revision)*. A requisition that's been approved (or partially approved, or moved into sourcing) can no longer be edited at all — silently changing an already-approved document's values/lines would undermine the approval just recorded against it. The Edit button itself only renders for draft/rejected statuses; the backend enforces the same rule independently. Need different terms after approval → raise a new requisition instead.
7. **Close** (`POST /pr/:id/close`, mandatory `reason`) *(new this revision)* — manually ends a requisition without further processing, at any point before it's already `closed`/`rejected` (a draft no longer needed, a submitted one withdrawn mid-approval, an approved one superseded, etc.) — distinct from Reject (pre-approval only) and from the *automatic* closure `refreshPrStatusAfterConsumption` already applies once every line is fully allocated to RFQ/PO. Releases any committed budget back to the cost center (`releaseCommitment`) so it doesn't sit stuck forever; cancels the live `workflow_instances` row (status → `cancelled`) if closed mid-approval, so the SLA escalation sweep stops flagging it. The reason is stored on `purchase_requisitions.closure_reason` and shown as a warning `Alert` on Overview — this is what distinguishes a manually-abandoned `closed` PR from a naturally-completed one, both of which share the same `closed` status value.

### 2.3 Budget Commitment Funnel

A PR's cost center budget moves through four stages, **reclassified** (subtracted from one stage, added to the next) rather than accumulated — so the same rupee is never simultaneously counted as committed *and* consumed *and* actual:

```
allocated_amount  (set once per cost center / fiscal year)
      │
      │  PR approved → commitBudget()
      ▼
committed_amount
      │
      │  PO created from the PR → releaseCommitment() + consumeBudget()
      ▼
consumed_amount
      │
      │  ASN posted to ERP → releaseConsumption() + recordActual()
      ▼
actual_amount

remaining = allocated − committed − consumed − actual
```

`allocated_amount`'s "set once per cost center / fiscal year" step now has an actual admin screen *(new)* — a **Budget Allocations** tab on System Settings (`POST`/`PUT /system/budget-allocations`, `system_admin`-only to create/edit, `system_admin`/`mdm_admin` read) lets an admin pick a cost center (reusing the same sub-master dropdown PR's Create form uses) and a fiscal year and set `allocated_amount`; `committed_amount`/`consumed_amount`/`actual_amount` are shown read-only on the same row since they're system-managed by the funnel above, not directly editable. Previously this row only ever existed via direct database seeding — see §5 #26.

A PO created against a **contract** additionally checks and updates the contract's own `consumed_value` / `remaining_value` in parallel (see §2.5).

### 2.4 Sourcing (RFQ) Flow

1. **Create** — from scratch or `POST /pr/:id/create-rfq` (carries department/priority/justification and line items/quantities from the PR).
2. **Vendor Suggestion** — `GET /rfq/vendor-suggestions/:itemMasterId` ranks candidate vendors for an item using three signals: item purchase history, `ProcurementInsightsService.getVendorScore()` (a blend of risk, price competitiveness, and contract status), and vendor risk level, producing an explainable `suggestion_score`. Rendered on the Create RFQ form's `VendorSuggestionPanel` — appears under a line the moment that line's item master is selected, sorted by `suggestion_score` DESC, with an "Add" button that pushes the vendor straight into the `vendor_ids` field.
3. **Publish** → vendors can now submit bids.
4. **Bid** (`POST /rfq/:id/bids`) — per line item: unit price, lead time, plus commercial terms (payment terms, warranty, taxes-included, deviations). Bids are scoped to the RFQ's **current round** (see below).
5. **Close** → no more bids accepted; non-responders marked `not_responded`.
6. **Multi-Round Negotiation** — a **Start New Round** action (Overview tab) calls `POST /rfq/:id/negotiate`, reopening a **closed** RFQ for a new round: increments `current_round`, sets status back to `negotiation`, re-invites the chosen vendors (defaults to whoever bid in the round just closed, editable in the panel) with a new deadline. A `negotiation`-status RFQ behaves like `published` for bidding/closing purposes — vendors can still submit bids and the admin can still close it to end the round.
   - Every prior round's bids persist permanently (the old "overwrite the same row" behavior is gone) — the **Negotiation History** tab (shown once `current_round > 1`) calls `GET /rfq/:id/negotiation-history` and renders each round's bids plus a cross-round price-trend table per vendor.
   - The RFQ can be closed and reopened for negotiation as many times as needed before award.
7. **Comparison** (`GET /rfq/:id/comparison`) — price matrix, historical benchmarks per line (now backed by the same item-identity benchmark `ProcurementInsightsService` uses elsewhere, not a separate calculation), vendor scorecards, TCO ranking, admin-configurable scoring weights. The backend also computes a **Should-Cost comparison** per bid item (deviation % from the item's market-average should-cost, flagged `high_deviation` above 15%) — rendered inline next to each price cell in the matrix as a colored deviation tag (green ≤5%, orange 5–15%, red >15%, with a warning icon once `high_deviation` is flagged).
8. **Award** (`POST /rfq/:id/award`) — single vendor or split across vendors per line; auto-generates the resulting PO(s) and records the awarded price into `price_history` for future benchmarking.

### 2.5 Purchase Order Flow

1. **Create** — manually, from an approved PR (`POST /pr/:id/create-po`), or from an awarded RFQ.
2. **Contract Consumption Tracking** — if the PO is created against a contract (directly, or via `CONTRACT_BASED` PR sourcing):
   - **Enforced** before creation: the contract must be `active`, within its validity window, and (if value-capped) this PO must not push consumption past `contract_value`.
   - **Updated** on creation: `consumed_value` increases by the PO's amount; `remaining_value` is always computed (`contract_value − consumed_value`), never stored, so the two numbers can't drift.
   - **Default pricing**: a PO line with no price of its own falls back to the contract's `default_unit_price`, if the contract has one.
3. **GST + HSN validation** — the PO's GSTIN (header) and every line's HSN/SAC code are format-checked at creation (15-char GSTIN pattern; 4/6/8-digit HSN/SAC) and at amendment time.
4. **PO Versioning / Amendment Workflow** — Purchase Orders gained a full detail view (it previously had none — list + create form only) to carry this: an **Amend PO** action proposes a change (validity date, terms of payment, incoterms, retention %, and/or existing lines' quantity/price/HSN/tax) via `POST /purchase-orders/:id/amend` — computes a JSON diff (`change_log`) against the current state and stores it as a new `po_versions` row, **without** touching the live PO yet (disabled while a proposal is already pending).
   - `POST /purchase-orders/:id/versions/:versionId/approve` (a *different* user than the proposer — self-approval is rejected, surfaced as a toast error) applies the change and bumps `purchase_orders.version`, available from the **Versions** tab.
   - `POST /purchase-orders/:id/versions/:versionId/reject` discards it, also from the Versions tab.
   - The Versions tab (`GET /purchase-orders/:id/versions`) shows the complete, permanent version/change-log history with a formatted field-by-field diff per version.
5. Fulfillment tracked against ASNs: each ASN line's shipped quantity adds to its matching `po_line_items.fulfilled_quantity` the moment the ASN posts to ERP (`POST /asns/:id/post`, gated on a completed GRN + matched Invoice — see §2.6), and `recomputePoFulfillmentStatus()` then rolls every line's fulfilled-vs-ordered quantity up into the PO's own `status`: `open` (nothing fulfilled yet) → `partially_fulfilled` (some lines short) → `fulfilled` (every line met). `closed` is a 4th status the schema reserves but nothing currently sets — there's no "close this PO" action anywhere yet (a real, open gap, not a documentation gap — see §6).

### 2.6 Shipment Flow — ASN → GRN → Invoice → ERP

This is the most structurally changed flow this iteration. The **ASN itself is unchanged** (still the vendor's shipment notice — LR/transporter/ETA, plus its own invoice_number/amount/tax fields, kept for backward compatibility) but two formal sub-documents now sit between it and ERP posting:

1. **ASN lifecycle (unchanged):** `Draft → Submitted → Validated → Posted`, with a `Rejected` branch.
2. **Goods Receipt Note (GRN)** — a **Create GRN** button on the ASN detail page (shown once the ASN is `validated` and no GRN exists yet) opens a panel capturing what was actually received and inspected per line: Received Qty and Rejected Qty inputs (Accepted Qty is shown computed, read-only) plus a Rejection Reason field, required whenever a line has a rejection. **Tolerance rule:** if the received quantity deviates from what the ASN said was shipped by more than `grn_quantity_tolerance_pct` (default 5%), or anything was rejected, that line is flagged `exceeds_tolerance` and the whole GRN goes to `exception` status — **auto-blocking** ERP posting (see step 4) and raising a `grn_tolerance_breach` exception. Once created, a read-only GRN card (status tag, per-line Shipped/Received/Accepted/Rejected/Tolerance columns) appears on the ASN detail page.
3. **Invoice** — a **Create Invoice** button (shown once a GRN exists and no invoice does yet) formalizes the ASN's own invoice fields into a dedicated record and runs the real **3-way match**: PO (ordered price) vs. GRN (accepted quantity, if one exists) vs. Invoice (billed price/quantity). A price deviation beyond `invoice_price_tolerance_pct` (default 2%), or invoiced quantity exceeding the GRN's accepted quantity, sets `match_status = blocked` and raises an `invoice_mismatch` exception — surfaced on the ASN detail page as a read-only Invoice card with a match-status tag and (when blocked) the specific reason.
4. **ERP Posting** (`POST /asns/:id/post`) — now requires (governed by the togglable `asn_require_grn_invoice_match` setting, default on) a **completed** GRN and a **matched** Invoice before it will post; only then does it release the PO-stage budget commitment into `actual_amount` (§2.3).

The pre-existing, simpler `PUT /asns/:id/three-way-match` (admin manually records matched/mismatched/pending + discrepancy reason, and the system independently re-checks PO-vs-ASN price/quantity) still exists unchanged alongside this — it's a lighter, ASN-only sanity check; the GRN/Invoice flow above is the more rigorous, formal gate.

### 2.7 Vendor Lifecycle Flow

```
Vendor created (admin) or self-registers
   │
   ▼
Draft ──► Submitted ──► Under Review ──► Approved ──► (Inactive, if deactivated)
   ▲                                         │
   └──────────── Rejected (revise+resubmit) ─┘
```

Running alongside `status`, a vendor's **`lifecycle_stage`** (Onboarding / Active / Dormant / Blocked) is *computed*, not set directly — from `status`, `blacklist_flag`, and **Vendor Compliance** state. All four of the following are now visible on the Vendor detail page's **Intelligence** tab:
- **Vendor Compliance** — the computed status (`GET /vendors/:id/compliance`) is rendered as a per-document table (expiry date, days remaining, OK/expiring-soon/expired status tag), plus a blocking `Alert` when the vendor is blacklisted or has an expired document; the raw `compliance_expiry_dates` label→date map remains separately editable on the Vendor Edit form. Each document's expiry is compared against a configurable alert window (`vendor_compliance_alert_days`, default 30); an expired document auto-blocks the vendor (`lifecycle_stage = blocked`) from being used in new RFQs/PRs, enforced via `assertVendorUsable()` at RFQ invite time and PR-to-PO conversion time, not just displayed.
- **Vendor Segmentation** — `vendor_segment` (`strategic` / `preferred` / `approved` / `tactical`) is now a Select field on the Vendor Edit form and a colored Tag on the Governance & Risk tab, used by RFQ vendor sorting (`sort_by_segment=true`) and by `computeSourcingRecommendation()` (a tactical preferred vendor pushes a PR toward RFQ_REQUIRED even below the value threshold; strategic/preferred push toward AUTO_PO).
- **Vendor 360 Profile** (`GET /vendors/:id/summary`, rendered on the Vendor detail page's main summary card) — `total_spend`, `active_po_count`, `on_time_delivery_pct`, `rejection_rate`, `last_transaction_date`, all computed live from PO/ASN history, nothing stored on the vendor record.
- **Supplier Risk Score** — the Intelligence tab also renders `ProcurementInsightsService.getVendorScore()` (Performance Score, Risk Level, **Risk Trend**, Price Competitiveness, Active Contract, plus the same actionable insights RFQ/PR see) rather than just the standalone Risk Dashboard. The underlying composite (7 weighted dimensions: delay, rejection, audit findings, financial, dependency, geographic, ESG) is recalculated on demand and still feeds the Risk Dashboard and the RFQ comparison engine's vendor scorecard too.

### 2.8 Exception Handling Flow

A single `procurement_exceptions` table is the central record for everything that's gone "not as expected" anywhere in the platform, surfaced end-to-end on the **Procurement Control Tower** page (`/exceptions`, see §5 #16). Every detector follows the same raise/auto-resolve pattern keyed by a `dedup_key`, so repeated checks never spam duplicate exceptions and a since-fixed problem clears itself automatically:

| Exception Type | Raised From | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| `budget_breach` | PR submit, PO creation | Requisition/PO value exceeds the cost center's remaining budget |
| `price_mismatch` | ASN three-way-match | Invoiced unit price differs from the PO unit price by more than 1% |
| `quantity_mismatch` | ASN three-way-match | Cumulative shipped quantity exceeds the PO line's ordered quantity |
| `vendor_risk` | Risk score recalculation | Vendor risk level is High, trend is worsening, or vendor is blacklisted |
| `compliance_expiry` | Vendor lifecycle sync | A compliance document has expired or is inside its alert window |
| `grn_tolerance_breach` | GRN creation | Received/rejected quantity outside tolerance of what was shipped |
| `invoice_mismatch` | Invoice creation | Price or quantity fails the 3-way match beyond tolerance |
| `sla_breach` | Workflow Engine escalation sweep | A pending approval is past its step's `sla_hours` |

The Control Tower consumes all of this: a summary row (Open/Critical/High/Medium/Low/Resolved Today, via a dedicated `GET /exceptions/summary` endpoint added for this page), filters by status/type/severity/module, a **View Source** action per row (vendor exceptions deep-link straight to `/vendors?id=`; every other module routes through `/traceability?id=` since GRN/Invoice exceptions' `record_id` is the GRN's/invoice's own id, not the parent ASN's, and Traceability resolves any of those ids to the right chain), and a **Resolve** action (`PUT /exceptions/:id/resolve`, remarks required) for open exceptions.

### 2.9 Workflow Engine — Conditional, Parallel, SLA-Escalating Approvals

The generic engine behind PR approval (§2.2) and reusable by any module:

- **Conditional steps** — a step's `condition_rule` (JSON array of `{field, operator, value}`, AND-combined; fields: `total_value`, `category`, `vendor_risk_level`) decides whether it applies to a given instance's context. No rule = always applies (fully backward compatible with every workflow defined before this existed).
- **Parallel approvals** — multiple steps can share a `step_order` (`is_parallel = true`); all of them must approve (AND-join) before the instance advances to the next wave; any one rejecting fails the whole instance immediately.
- **SLA escalation** — each step's `sla_hours` (always existed, was previously unused) now computes a `sla_due_at` on the per-wave `workflow_instance_step_approvals` row; a sweep (`POST /workflow/escalations/check`, also run lazily whenever the Instances list loads) flags overdue approvals, raises an `sla_breach` exception, and notes the step's configured `escalation_role` for visibility.
- If every step on a resolved workflow is conditional and none apply to a given instance, the instance auto-resolves with zero human approvals — both PR's submit flow and the line-level approval endpoints handle this correctly (finalizing the PR immediately, or as soon as the last blocking line is resolved) rather than leaving the document stuck with no actionable next step.

### 2.10 Field Configuration — Conditional Mandatory Fields & Role-Based Visibility

- **Conditional Mandatory Fields** — a `field_requirements` row's effective "is this required" = its static `is_mandatory` flag **OR** a matching `condition_rule` (same shape/evaluator as Workflow Engine's, shared via `backend/src/common/conditions.js`). Example wired end-to-end: RFQ's `description` field (doubling as written justification) becomes mandatory once the RFQ's `budget_value` exceeds ₹10,00,000 — evaluated live as the admin types, via `GET /system/field-config/rfq?total_value=...`.
- **Role-Based Visibility** — a field's `visible_roles` (null = everyone) is exposed via a *separate* endpoint (`GET /system/field-config/:module/visibility`) and a *separate* hook function (`isVisible()`), so the existing mandatory-flag contract used by 12 pages is completely unaffected. **No page currently calls `isVisible()` to actually hide a field** — the capability exists end-to-end (backend + hook + an admin editor on System Settings) but isn't consumed by any specific form yet.

### 2.11 Procurement OS Event Flow

A durable, in-process event backbone (`backend/src/common/eventBus.js`) introduced for the Procurement OS expansion (§3) — deliberately **not** Kafka/RabbitMQ: a single Node process against a single database doesn't need a broker, and adding one would be operational complexity with no corresponding benefit here. `emitEvent(type, payload)` does two things synchronously: persists a row to `event_log` (so "did X happen" survives a restart and is queryable — see `GET /api/events/log`), then dispatches to any in-process subscribers registered via `onEvent(type, handlerName, handler)`. A subscriber's failure (sync throw or rejected promise) is caught and logged, never propagated back into the business logic that emitted the event — a bad SAP-sync listener can't fail a PR approval.

Four points in the existing flow now emit an event, each picked as the closest real equivalent where this app's lifecycle doesn't have a literal "approved" step:

| Event | Emitted From | Equivalent To |
|---|---|---|
| `PR_APPROVED` | `finalizePrApproval()` (`pr.helpers.js`) | A PR's workflow finishing with `approved` or `partially_approved` |
| `PO_APPROVED` | `createPoFromPr()` and the manual `POST /purchase-orders` (`po.routes.js`) | PO creation — this app's PO lifecycle has no separate approval step |
| `GRN_COMPLETED` | `createGrn()` (`grn.service.js`), only when the GRN has no tolerance breach | A clean (non-`exception`) GRN |
| `INVOICE_APPROVED` | `createInvoiceFromAsn()` (`invoice.service.js`), only when `match_status = 'matched'` | A clean 3-way match — invoices have no separate approval step either |

Two modules currently subscribe, each independently, to these events — neither needs to know the other exists:
- **Payments** (`payments.service.js`) subscribes to `INVOICE_APPROVED` → auto-creates a `payment_schedule` row due `payment_terms_days` (setting, default 30) after the invoice date, plus the invoice's own `vendor_ledger` debit entry.
- **Inventory** (`inventory.service.js`) subscribes to `GRN_COMPLETED` → walks each accepted line back to its Item Master record (`grn_line_items.po_line_id → po_line_items.pr_line_item_id → pr_line_items.item_master_id`) and adds the accepted quantity to `inventory_stock`, skipping any line with no Item Master link.
- **The (mocked) SAP Connector** (`sap-connector.service.js`) subscribes to all four named events plus `PAYMENT_COMPLETED`, pushing each to the mock SAP service and logging the outcome to `integration_logs` — see §3's Procurement OS subsection for the retry/DLQ behavior on failure.

### 2.12 Transactional Integrity — `withTransaction()` *(new)*

A real bug, reported live: creating a vendor whose email collided with an existing login returned a 500 — but the vendor row had *already been inserted* before the second insert (the login) failed, leaving an orphaned, login-less "ghost" vendor that didn't show up anywhere usable. Tracing it further found the same risk was systemic: **no route or service anywhere in the live API ever opened a real database transaction** — `beginTransaction`/`getConnection()` existed only in one-time seed scripts. Every multi-step write (header + line items, delete-then-reinsert, propose-then-apply) was a sequence of independent `pool.query()` calls, each on whichever connection the pool happened to hand out — a failure partway through left whatever had already run committed, regardless of the overall request's reported success or failure.

This was *not* a missing-plumbing problem: nearly every helper function across `pr.helpers.js`, `company.helpers.js`, `contracts.service.js`, `workflow-engine.service.js`, `exceptions.service.js`, etc. already accepted an optional trailing `conn` parameter (the `const c = conn || pool` convention used throughout this codebase, originally built for exactly this purpose) — route handlers simply never passed a real one through.

**The fix** — `backend/src/common/db.js`'s `withTransaction(fn)`: acquires a dedicated connection, runs `fn(conn)`, commits on success, rolls back and re-throws (preserving the original error's type/statusCode, so `ValidationError`/`ConflictError` etc. still resolve to the right HTTP status) on failure, and always releases the connection. Every multi-write handler across the app now wraps its writes in this and threads the resulting `conn` into every helper call:

| Module | Wrapped |
|---|---|
| Vendor | `POST /` (create + login), `POST /import` (per-row), `PUT /:id`, `PUT /:id/onboarding` (header + addresses + bank accounts), `DELETE /:id` (cascade) |
| PR | `POST /` (create), `PUT /:id` (edit), `POST /:id/submit`, `POST /:id/approve`, `POST /:id/reject`, `POST /:id/close`, line-level approve/reject, `POST /:id/create-rfq`, `POST /:id/create-po` |
| RFQ | `POST /` (create), `PUT /:id` (edit), `POST /:id/bids`, `POST /:id/negotiate`, `POST /:id/award` (multi-vendor split award) |
| Purchase Orders | `POST /` (manual create), amendment propose/approve/reject |
| ASN | `POST /` (create), `POST /:id/post` (ERP posting + PO fulfillment rollup + budget actualization), `POST /:id/grn`, `POST /:id/invoice` |
| Workflow Engine | `POST /` (create + steps), `PUT /:id` (edit + steps), `POST /instances/:id/advance`, the SLA escalation sweep (one transaction *per overdue row*, not one for the whole sweep — a bad row can't undo others already escalated) |
| Tickets | `POST /` (create + vendor assignment), `PUT /:id/vendors/:vendorId/close`, `PUT /:id/reassign` |

Also added alongside this: `vendor.routes.js` now checks `users.email` (not just `vendors.email`) before creating a vendor, turning the most common collision into a clean 409 instead of relying on rollback alone; and `vendor_addresses`/`vendor_bank_accounts` rows (several NOT NULL columns — `city`/`state`/`country`/`pin_code` for addresses, plus `ifsc_code`/`account_number`/etc. for bank accounts) are now validated by field name on both the backend (`validateAddressRows`/`validateBankAccountRows`) and the frontend (`Vendors.jsx` and `VendorOnboarding.jsx`, mirroring the same checks) before submission — previously an incomplete row only failed once it hit the database's own constraint, as the same kind of opaque 500.

**A second, related but distinct bug** surfaced while investigating a user report ("can't see this vendor, but the system says it already exists"): 9 `users` rows with `role='vendor'` whose `vendor_id` pointed to a vendor that didn't exist in `vendors` at all — orphaned logins from this exact failure mode, predating the fix, silently blocking those emails from ever being used again. Found via `SELECT u.* FROM users u WHERE u.role='vendor' AND NOT EXISTS (SELECT 1 FROM vendors v WHERE v.id = u.vendor_id)`; confirmed each had zero other references anywhere in the schema before removing them (user-confirmed before deletion).

### Decision Intelligence Layer

A layer of computed insight and automated detection sitting on top of the transactional modules above, rather than a module of its own:

Surfaced via a reusable `SmartAssistantPanel` on PR's Intelligence tab, RFQ's Overview tab (admin roles only), and Vendors' Intelligence tab; extended to a 4th entity type, `po` (`getPoAssistant` — vendor score + pending-amendment note + open exceptions) — not mounted as its own standalone `SmartAssistantPanel` on the PO page, but reached indirectly the moment the PO Detail page's `DecisionPanel` (`entityType="po"`) renders, since `getDecisionPanel('po', id)` routes straight to `getPoAssistant` internally (§5 #13). Gated by `smart_assistant_enabled` — off, the panel renders nothing and the backend returns an empty insight list rather than an error.
- **`DecisionPanel`** *(new)* — a presentational regrouping of the same Smart Assistant data above into 4 fixed sections (Critical Alerts / Risks / Cost Saving Opportunities / Recommendations) via `GET /assistant/decision-panel/:entityType/:id` → `SmartAssistantService.getDecisionPanel()`. Buckets purely by `source`/`type` (no new computation): `source: 'exception'` items always become Critical Alerts; everything else sorts by `type` (`risk`/`compliance` → Risks, `cost_saving` → Cost Saving Opportunities, `recommendation` → Recommendations). Rendered as a sticky right-rail card on the PR, RFQ, and PO Detail pages (`entityType="pr"|"rfq"|"po"`); on RFQ it's admin-only (matching `SmartAssistantPanel`'s own role gating there). Not yet mounted on the Vendor detail page.
- **ProcurementInsightsService** — item price benchmarking (market avg/min/max/last, vendor breakdown, cost-deviation vs. standard cost), should-cost comparison (deviation % + high-deviation warning against historical data), vendor scoring (blends risk + price competitiveness + contract status into one explainable score), and RFQ vendor suggestion. Every one of these is now rendered somewhere: PR's Intelligence tab, Item Master's Price Insights tab, Vendors' Intelligence tab, the Vendor Portal Performance page, RFQ's `VendorSuggestionPanel` (Create form) and should-cost deviation tags (Comparison tab price matrix), and indirectly as `cost_saving`/`risk` insights via the Smart Assistant. No remaining "computed but never shown" gap for this service — see §6 for what's still open elsewhere.
- **Vendor Risk Actionability** *(new)* — `GET /vendors/:id/risk-actions` turns `calculateVendorRiskScore()` + `getComplianceStatus()` into concrete, rule-based recommendations (`replace_vendor` when rejection score dominates the risk level, with up to 5 same-category alternative vendors suggested; `reduce_dependency` when ≥60 open-PO concentration risk; `trigger_audit` when audit findings, blacklist, or an expired compliance document are present) rather than leaving a risk score visible but inert. Rendered as a "Recommended Actions" card on the Vendor Intelligence tab, fetched alongside the existing compliance/score calls.
- **Exception Management Engine** — the eight detectors in §2.8, fully surfaced on the **Procurement Control Tower** page (`/exceptions`) with summary counts, filtering, and resolution — plus two new tabs: **Budget Health** (`GET /exceptions/budget-health`, every cost center's allocated/committed/consumed/actual/remaining with a utilization `Progress` bar) and **Vendor Risk & Opportunities** (`GET /exceptions/vendor-risk` — top high-risk vendors; `GET /assistant/cost-saving-opportunities` — `cost_saving`-type Smart Assistant insights scanned across the 25 most recently submitted open requisitions, reusing `getPrAssistant` per PR rather than a second detector).
- **Traceability Engine** — `getFullTraceability(documentId)` walks the complete PR→RFQ→PO→ASN→GRN/Invoice chain (using the existing `document_flow_mapping` ledger plus direct FKs) and returns documents, edges, and a timeline, rendered on the **Document Traceability** page (`/traceability`) as a clickable flow diagram plus timeline.

### Procurement OS Layer *(new)*

A set of modules extending the platform from a procurement tool toward a broader "Procurement OS," built entirely inside the existing app and database — no new services, no message broker, no separate database (an explicit constraint for this expansion, not a limitation discovered after the fact). All thirteen pieces of the originating brief, mapped onto this codebase:

- **Multi-Company + Intercompany** (`backend/src/modules/company/`) — `organization_master` → `company_master` → `business_unit_master`, plus `company_sap_mapping` for the (mocked) SAP connector below. `purchase_requisitions`/`purchase_orders`/`asns` each gained nullable `organization_id`/`company_id`/`business_unit_id` columns (additive — every pre-existing row and insert path is unaffected). **Intercompany**: a vendor can be flagged `vendors.internal_company_id` (it's one of the organization's *own* companies); a PO created against such a vendor automatically mirrors into a `sales_orders` row in the seller company's books (`maybeCreateIntercompanySalesOrder()`, called from both PO-creation paths) — best-effort, since a mirroring failure must never roll back the real PO. CRUD: `GET/POST/PUT /api/companies`, `/api/companies/:id/business-units`, `/api/companies/:id/sap-mapping`, `GET /api/companies/sales-orders`.
- **Payment Lifecycle** (`backend/src/modules/payments/`) — `payment_schedule` (auto-created off `INVOICE_APPROVED`, §2.11), `payments` (one row per payment — a schedule can be paid in several installments, not just once), `vendor_ledger` (running per-vendor debit/credit balance: an invoice debits, a payment credits), `cashflow_projection` (a cache table rebuilt on demand from outstanding schedules, bucketed by due date). A **Payment Run** (`POST /api/payments/run`) pays a batch of due schedules in one call; **aging** (`GET /api/payments/aging`) buckets overdue amounts 0–30/31–60/61–90/90+ days per vendor; **reconciliation** (`PUT /api/payments/:id/reconcile`) is a manual mark-as-settled (the mock bank doesn't push anything back on its own — see the SAP connector's `pullPaymentStatusFromSap` for the inbound-pull equivalent).
- **Inventory Integration** (`backend/src/modules/inventory/`) — `warehouses`, `inventory_stock` (qty-on-hand + reorder level/quantity per warehouse/item), `stock_movements` (an append-only ledger of every in/out, mirroring `vendor_ledger`'s pattern). Stock increases automatically off `GRN_COMPLETED` (§2.11); `POST /api/inventory/consume` is the manual stock-out (issuing material to a department), and if the resulting balance drops below `reorder_level`, it auto-creates a **draft** Purchase Requisition (`maybeCreateReorderDraftPr()`) — deliberately left in `draft` status (a placeholder for procurement to review and submit through the *existing* PR submit flow, with its budget checks and approval routing, not a parallel one) and deduped so repeated consumption under threshold doesn't spam a new draft every call.
- **SAP Connector (mocked)** (`backend/src/modules/sap-connector/`) — `sap-mock.service.js` simulates the "SAP side" entirely (per the brief's own Module 13 ask for a SAP simulation; there's no real SAP system to integrate with). `sap-connector.service.js` subscribes to the four named events (§2.11) and pushes each through `withRetry()` (3 attempts, linear backoff) before logging the outcome to `integration_logs`; a sync that still fails after 3 attempts lands in `integration_dlq` for manual retry (`POST /api/integration/dlq/:id/retry`). Bi-directional vendor sync (`pushVendorToSap`/`pullVendorFromSap`) and an inbound payment-status pull (`pullPaymentStatusFromSap`, writing through the same single path `payments.service.js`'s own sync function uses) exist as callable backend functions and routes — **no frontend screen calls these two yet**, see §6.
- **Decision Engine expansion** (`backend/src/modules/decision-engine/`) — `decision_rules` (admin-defined: a module + the same `{field, operator, value}` condition shape Workflow Engine and Field Configuration already use, reused via `common/conditions.js` rather than a third dialect + an `output_type`/`output_template`) evaluated against live context; every match is both returned and permanently recorded to `decision_outputs`. Wired into `POST /pr/:id/submit` today (passing `total_value`/`cost_center`/`budget_remaining_pct`/`sourcing_strategy` as context) — a no-op until an admin actually defines a rule for the `pr` module.
- **Next Best Action engine** (`backend/src/modules/action-engine/`) — "pull" style by design (computed live from a record's current state, not stored), so it plugs directly into the existing `SmartAssistantService`/`DecisionPanel` Recommendations bucket instead of needing a UI of its own. Ships with the brief's own worked example as a concrete built-in check (`getNextBestActionsForPr`: PR approved + `RFQ_REQUIRED` + no RFQ created yet → recommends `create_rfq`), plus evaluation of any admin-defined `action_rules` for the same `PR_APPROVED` trigger event.
- **Security — multi-company access** (`company.helpers.js`'s `getUserCompanyIds()`) — `system_admin`/`mdm_admin` keep the same top-level-override convention used everywhere else in this app (workflow steps, field visibility); every other role only sees `company_master` rows it has an explicit `user_company_access` grant for (`GET/POST/DELETE /api/companies/user-access`). Returns `null` to mean "unrestricted" and an empty array (never confused with `null`) to mean "no grants yet" — the distinction matters because an empty array silently hiding every company from a legitimately unrestricted role would be a real bug.
- **Error handling — retry + dead-letter queue** (`backend/src/common/retry.js`) — generic, not SAP-specific (anything could call `withRetry()`); a permanent failure writes one `integration_dlq` row and re-throws so the caller's own log entry still records it accurately.
- **Logging & monitoring** — `integration_logs` (every connector attempt: type, direction, request/response payload, status, attempt count) and a separate, generic `audit_logs` (actor/action/module/record/before-after JSON — distinct from PR's own narrative `pr_audit_log`; wired into the company create/update routes today as the first consumer, via `common/auditLog.js`'s `recordAudit()`).
- **Testing** — every module above shipped with its own Jest unit-test file (mocked `pool`, no live DB needed) covering the success path, the documented edge cases (idempotency, insufficient stock, already-resolved DLQ entries, synchronous subscriber throws), and at least one failure path. 64 backend tests total as of this revision (`cd backend && npm test` — includes `common/db.test.js` for `withTransaction()`, §2.12); all new code was also exercised against the live database end-to-end (full PR→PO→ASN→GRN→Invoice→Payment→Reconciliation chains, intercompany mirroring, auto-reorder, decision/action rule firing) before being considered done.

None of this required Kafka, RabbitMQ, or a second database/service — every module reads/writes the same `vendor_portal` MySQL database the rest of the app already uses, behind plain Express routes in the same process, per explicit product direction for this expansion.

## 4. Global Design System

### 4.1 Stack & Philosophy

- **Component library:** Ant Design 5, default theme tokens — no `ConfigProvider` override; all customization lives in one global stylesheet (`frontend/src/global.css`).
- **Charts:** Recharts (Pie/Bar).
- **Icons:** `@ant-design/icons` exclusively.
- **No dark mode** — the Sider is the only permanently-dark surface.
- **"No Drawer/Modal" is now actually true, app-wide.** Every page was audited this iteration and confirmed to have **zero** remaining `<Drawer>`/`<Modal>` usages. The three sanctioned patterns are:
  1. **Full-page view-state swaps** (`'list' | 'detail' | 'edit' | 'create'`) for primary record flows — Vendors, ASNs, RFQ, PR, PurchaseOrders (gained a `'list' | 'detail'` swap this pass — see §5 #13), ItemMaster, Contracts, Tickets, AuditManagement (checklists).
  2. **`InlineExpandPanel`** (`frontend/src/components/ui/InlineExpandPanel.jsx`) — renders inline in normal page flow with a left accent border, replacing every former Drawer/Modal for forms and confirmations-needing-input.
  3. **`SplitScreenLayout`** (`frontend/src/components/ui/SplitScreenLayout.jsx`) — list+detail side by side; used by Tickets and WorkflowEngine's Instances tab specifically (Vendors/ItemMaster were *moved away* from this pattern earlier, back to full-page list→detail navigation via a zoom-icon "View" action, per explicit product feedback that the side-by-side detail pane didn't read well for those two).

### 4.2 `PageHeader` — the new shared header component

`frontend/src/components/ui/PageHeader.jsx` — breadcrumb trail (Home icon + segments, optionally clickable) + title/subtitle + optional Back button + a right-aligned actions slot. Used by 21 of 28 pages: **Contracts, WorkflowEngine, ItemMaster, UserManagement, DocumentCenter, VendorOnboarding, ESGTracking, ExtractionConfig, AuditManagement, SystemSettings, ExceptionsDashboard, TraceabilityView, PurchaseOrders** (13, from earlier passes — see §5 for each), the three new **VendorPortalDashboard, VendorPortalPerformance, VendorPortalTransactions** pages (built with `PageHeader` from the start), plus **PR, RFQ, Vendors, ASNs, Tickets** — these last five render `PageHeader` only while `ui_improvements_enabled` is on (§4.9); with the flag off they fall back to their original hand-rolled "Back button + Title" header, unchanged. The remaining pages (**Dashboard, RiskDashboard, PriceBenchmarking, Sub Masters ×2, Login, Change Password**) don't use `PageHeader` at all yet — a known, low-risk consistency gap, see §6.

### 4.3 Page Structure Convention (the four rules applied this iteration)

Every page touched this iteration now has, to the extent its content supports it:

1. **Header + breadcrumb** — via `PageHeader` (new pages) or the pre-existing Back+Title pattern (untouched pages).
2. **Summary panel** — a `Row` of `Statistic`-in-`Card` tiles near the top of the list/detail view, computed from real data (not placeholders) — e.g. Contracts' Total/Active/Total Value/Total Remaining, ItemMaster's Total Items/With Standard Cost/Without Category, WorkflowEngine Instances' Total/In Progress.
3. **Tabs** — for any page with more than one facet of content (ItemMaster: Overview/Price Insights/Preferred Vendors; Contracts: Overview/Consumption). Pages whose content is genuinely single-purpose (Contracts' old create-only form, ExtractionConfig) use a list + `InlineExpandPanel` instead, which is treated as an accepted alternative, not a violation. Wizard-style flows (ASN, VendorOnboarding) use `Steps` as their established alternative to Tabs.
4. **Insights / Alerts / Recommendations** — every page touched this iteration surfaces at least one data-driven `Alert` or insight panel: Contracts (near-expiry / low-remaining-value), WorkflowEngine (SLA breach + parallel-wave status), ItemMaster (price benchmark insight + cost-deviation tag), UserManagement (no-MDM-admin-configured warning), DocumentCenter (pending-verification count), ESGTracking (non-compliant-vendor warning), VendorOnboarding (status-driven alert replacing a plain text status line).

### 4.4 Overlays — Drawer vs. Modal (historical note)

Both overlay types were previously in use without a documented rule for which to pick (Drawer for most secondary actions, Modal only in the PR/RFQ/PurchaseOrders sourcing-chain trio). **This is now moot** — every former Drawer/Modal has been converted to `InlineExpandPanel`, full-page views, or `SplitScreenLayout`, per §4.1.

### 4.5 Multi-Step Wizards

Unchanged: ASN (4 steps, gated — Next validates only the current step) and Vendor Onboarding (5 steps, free-clickable, validated only on final submit) remain the only two `Steps`-based wizards, with the same validation-strictness inconsistency between them noted previously.

### 4.6 Tables & Filtering

Unchanged conventions: filter `Card` + `Row` of inputs above the table, ending in Search/Clear; table `size` split between `middle` (primary lists) and `small` (nested tables); row-click-to-detail via `onRow`; `Popconfirm` (not a Drawer) for one-click destructive actions; no shared `<FilterBar>` or `<RepeatableList>` component — each page still hand-rolls these.

**Column sorting & header filters** *(new this revision)* — every data grid in the app (PR, RFQ, PO, ASN, Vendors, Tickets, Item Master, Contracts, Audit, Control Tower, Workflow Engine, User Management, Risk, Price Benchmarking, ESG, Document Center, Vendor Portal Transactions, and every table on System Settings, including the new Budget Allocations one) now has a `sorter` on its textual/numeric/date columns and AntD's column-header `filters`/`onFilter` dropdown on its enum-like columns (status, priority, severity, role, category, module). This sits on top of, not in place of, each page's own toolbar filter `Card` — the toolbar filters re-query the server with new params; the column-header filters operate client-side on whatever page of rows is currently loaded. Still no shared sorter/filter helper — each column's comparator is written inline, following the existing per-page convention above.

### 4.7 Status & Color System

Still one local `*_COLOR` constant per page rather than a shared palette file. The cross-module rule still mostly holds (green = good/approved, red = bad/rejected, blue = neutral/in-motion, orange = needs attention, grey = inert) with the same known exception: **PR colors `submitted` as orange and its own `approved` as blue**, where every other module uses blue-for-submitted and green-for-approved. New this iteration, added without introducing a new inconsistency:
- `APPROVAL_STATUS_COLOR` (WorkflowEngine, per-wave approvals) — pending=orange, approved=green, rejected=red (consistent with the cross-module rule).
- Contracts' alert tags — `Expiring` = orange, `Low value` = red (consistent).

### 4.8 Iconography, Buttons, Charts, Empty States, Responsive Grid

Unchanged from the prior design reference — `@ant-design/icons` exclusively, `type="primary"` reserved for the one forward-progress action per view, `dashed` for "add another row," toast-only feedback (still skewed heavily toward `message.error`), Recharts at a fixed 260px height, centered-text empty states, fixed-span `Col` grids assuming a desktop viewport.

### 4.9 Feature Flags

A minimal, additive toggle mechanism introduced this revision — deliberately built on infrastructure that already existed rather than a new flags table/API:

- **Storage** — each flag is one row in the pre-existing `system_settings` table (`setting_key`/`setting_value`), seeded by `migrate-feature-flags.js` (idempotent `INSERT … WHERE NOT EXISTS`, all three default to `'true'`). No schema change.
- **Backend read** — `backend/src/common/featureFlags.js` exports `isFeatureEnabled(key, conn)`, reading the same table the admin-facing Module/Procurement Settings already manage. A missing row is treated as **enabled** — a flag can only ever turn a capability *off*, never silently hide one whose row was never seeded.
- **Backend gate** — the `assistant` and `vendor-portal` route modules check their flag via `router.use()` middleware and respond `{ success: true, data: null/empty, feature_enabled: false }` when off, rather than 404ing or 403ing — a disabled flag should never look like a broken endpoint to anything calling it directly.
- **Frontend read** — `frontend/src/contexts/FeatureFlagsContext.jsx` (`FeatureFlagsProvider`, wraps `AppLayout` alongside `FieldConfigProvider`) fetches all three via the existing `GET /system/settings/:key` (already open to any authenticated role) once per session; `useFeatureFlag(key)` returns `true` while loading, on error, or for an unrecognized key — same "only ever turns something off" guarantee as the backend.
- **The three flags:**
  | Flag | Gates |
  |---|---|
  | `smart_assistant_enabled` | `SmartAssistantPanel` rendering (PR/RFQ/Vendor) and the `/api/assistant/*` routes' actual computation |
  | `vendor_portal_v2_enabled` | The "My Portal" sidebar group for the vendor role, and the `/api/vendor-portal/*` routes' actual computation |
  | `ui_improvements_enabled` | Whether PR/RFQ/Vendors/ASNs/Tickets render `PageHeader` (§4.2) or their original header; also read by the new, currently-unadopted `StatusTag.jsx` (falls back to a plain untagged `<Tag>` when off) |
- **An admin toggles a flag** the same way as any other setting — `PUT /system/settings { key, value }` (`system_admin`-only) — no redeploy, no separate flags UI.
- **Lifecycle stage** *(new)* — each flag's `setting_value` optionally carries a stage suffix, `'true:beta'`/`'false:experimental'`/etc. (`backend/src/common/featureFlags.js`'s `parseValue()`; a bare `'true'`/`'false'` with no suffix parses as `lifecycle: 'stable'`, so every flag from before this addition keeps working unparsed). Three stages: **Experimental** (`isFeatureEnabled(key, conn, role)` additionally enforces `system_admin`-only visibility when a role is passed — most call sites don't pass one, so this is opt-in per caller), **Beta** (visible to everyone, no extra restriction — where all three current flags sit today, migrated by `migrate-feature-flag-lifecycle.js`), and **Stable** (the intended end state once a flag is validated, at which point removing the flag check from the gated code becomes its own small, isolated cleanup). `GET /system/feature-flags/status` (`system_admin`/`mdm_admin`) lists every flag's `{key, enabled, lifecycle}`; a **Feature Flags** tab on System Settings lets an admin change either independently (a `Switch` for enabled, a `Select` for lifecycle) via the same `PUT /system/settings`.

### 4.10 Decision-First Page Pattern

Introduced on the Purchase Requisition module (§5 #8) as the reference implementation for "tell the user what matters, don't make them go find it" — intended to be the template the next module redesign reuses, not a PR-only one-off:

- **List view** — an **Insight** column (one ranked, color-coded badge per row — "N line(s) need approval," "Pending Approval," "Budget Exceeded," "RFQ Required — Not Created," "Ready for PO," "Completed" — first matching rule wins, computed from two new read-only subqueries on the list endpoint, `rfq_line_count`/`blocking_line_count`, plus one bulk `GET /exceptions` call for open `budget_breach` exceptions — no N+1 per-row calls); a left-accent **row highlight** (`row-highlight-critical`/`-warning`/`-healthy` in `global.css`) matching the same severity; a row-**expand preview** (top 3 line items, budget status, a one-click "suggested next action" button, lazy-fetched via the existing `GET /pr/:id` on first expand); and an **Actions** column redesigned to one primary button (the recommended action — a real one-click Approve via `Popconfirm` where that's already wired, otherwise a navigation to detail) plus a `MoreOutlined` overflow `Dropdown` for everything else, replacing the previous 5-button row.

- **Create view** — converted from one long scrolling form into a 4-step `Steps` wizard (**Basic Info → Line Items → Review & Insights → Submit**) sharing one `createForm` instance throughout (AntD's default `preserve: true` keeps every step's values intact even while other steps are conditionally unmounted — the same trick the pre-existing Basic Information/Smart Controls split already relied on), each step gated by a lightweight `validateFields()` check before advancing. ### 4.11 Sidebar Navigation & the Masters Group *(new)*

`frontend/src/components/Layout/Sidebar.jsx` builds one of four distinct `Menu` item arrays based on `user.role` (`vendorItems` / `systemAdminItems` / `adminItems` for `mdm_admin` / `procurementItems`) — a genuinely different menu per role, not one menu with items conditionally hidden. ## 7. Appendix: Schema Additions This Iteration

Two of them needed an in-place ordering fix to actually run clean: a migration that drops an index and adds its replacement must `ADD` the replacement *before* it `DROP`s the original, or MySQL rejects the `DROP` with `ER_DROP_INDEX_FK` whenever the original index backs a foreign key — found and fixed in `migrate-rfq-negotiation.js` (`uq_rfq_bid` → `uq_rfq_bid_round`) and `migrate-workflow-engine-enhancements.js` (`uq_workflow_step_order` → `idx_workflow_step_order`).

The UI pass that built the Control Tower, Traceability view, GRN/Invoice capture, RFQ Negotiation UI, PO Amendment UI, and Vendor Intelligence tab added **zero new migrations** — it's entirely additive frontend work plus one new backend read endpoint (`GET /exceptions/summary`, an aggregate query over the existing `procurement_exceptions` table) and an extension of the Traceability queries to also walk `goods_receipt_notes`/`invoices` (tables already created by `migrate-asn-grn-invoice.js` above).

The Smart Procurement Assistant / Vendor Portal 2.0 / Feature Flags pass likewise added **no schema beyond the one settings-row seed above** — `backend/src/services/SmartAssistantService.js` and the new `modules/assistant/` and `modules/vendor-portal/` route modules are pure read-only aggregations over tables every earlier migration already created (`procurement_exceptions`, `vendor_compliance_alert_days`, `rfq_vendors`, `vendor_bids`/`vendor_bid_items`, `invoices`, `purchase_orders`, `asns`).

This fifth revision's work (PO fulfillment-status rollup, the Budget Allocations screen, the Reports module, and universal grid sorting/filtering) likewise added **no new migrations** — `recomputePoFulfillmentStatus()` and the Budget Allocations routes both read/write columns `migrate-budget-commitment.js`/the original schema already created, and Reports is a pure read layer over every existing table it reports on.

**Seed scripts** — alongside `migrate-procurement-os.js` above, this revision added `backend/src/config/seed-procurement-os.js` (Item Master — which, like Vendors/PRs, never had a seed script at all before this; a 2nd company + business unit + SAP mapping for the intercompany demo; one vendor flagged `internal_company_id`; `inventory_stock` reorder levels for every seeded item; two example `decision_rules` and one example `action_rules` row), now chained into `npm run seed`. Doing this surfaced one real idempotency bug in the *existing* `seed.js`: its vendor-seeding loop checked `vendors.email` to decide whether to (re)create a vendor, but always unconditionally tried to `INSERT` a matching `users` row — fine the first time, but a duplicate-key failure on any later reseed where `vendors` was truncated without also truncating `users` (exactly the scenario this revision's own full database wipe-and-reseed exercised). Fixed by checking `users.email` first and re-pointing an existing login at the freshly-recreated vendor row instead of trying to insert a second one. The full `npm run migrate && npm run seed` pipeline was run twice in a row after the fix, back-to-back, specifically to confirm it's now genuinely idempotent end to end, not just "doesn't error the first time."
